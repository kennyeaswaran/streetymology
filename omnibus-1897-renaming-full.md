# Omnibus: the 1897 renaming ordinance — full-text hunt + companion documents

**⚠ STATUS (2026-07-04): the full ordinance text is NOT in CDNC.** A systematic
sweep (search record below) shows the Los Angeles Herald never printed the
complete list — only the summary (Feb. 2), the committee's amendments (Feb. 21,
banked in omnibus-1897-renaming.md), and session-by-session coverage. The
Herald's own Mar. 5 article says the ordinance made **326 changes** (not
"350+") and that the complete old→new data went into a Directory-company map,
not the paper. This file banks (a) the negative search result so nobody re-runs
it, (b) every companion document found, with all itemized changes transcribed,
and (c) where the full text most likely lives.

Status markers: ☐ unprocessed · ✔ applied to data · ⏸ out of coverage · † street gone/absorbed

## Timeline of the ordinance

- **Feb. 1** — Mayor transmits the commission's report + ordinance + red-ink map
  to council. Commission: Gen. Charles Forman (chairman), Dana H. Burks
  (secretary), Charles F. Lummis, [J. F.?] Washburn, Prof. J. M. Guinn.
  Six weeks in session.
- **Feb. 2** — Council adopts under suspension of the rules (Nickell alone
  voting no).
- **Feb. 8** — Mayor Snyder returns it unsigned (veto); council reconsiders,
  defers to Feb. 15 for protests.
- **Feb. 15** — protests referred to a joint special council committee +
  commission (Councilman Toll chairman).
- **Feb. 18** — joint session hears protestants item by item (Feb. 19 article).
- **Feb. 21 (Sunday)** — Herald prints the joint committee's final report
  (~50 amendments).
- **Feb. 23** — Council hears remaining protests and passes the ordinance as
  amended, "with but one or two unimportant changes"; Georgia Bell → Georgia.
- **Feb. 26** — Mayor Snyder signs; "it is now the law."
- **Mar. 1897** — Directory company's Burks map (the 326 old→new changes) goes
  to the lithographer.
- **Oct. 25 / Dec. 27** — city engineer's supplementary renaming ordinance
  proposed / adopted (list never printed in Herald).

## Search record — why the Herald is ruled out

All searches on CDNC (cdnc.ucr.edu), year 1897, both Herald-only and
all-publications; a printed 326-item list (even a badly-OCR'd legal ad) would
surface at least one of these distinctive names, and none do:

- **Ugarte** — 2 hits, both known (Feb. 9 veto, Feb. 21 report)
- **Micheltorena** — Herald hits are 2 Santa Barbara news items only
- **Zalvidea** — 0 hits statewide
- **Cimarron** — only the veto (plus unrelated Oklahoma flood news)
- **Arapahoe** (Feb. 25–Mar. 31) — 0 hits
- **Queirolo** — 24 hits statewide, none an ordinance printing (Aug. 3
  "WATER REPORT" shows "Queirolo street" in use — Ash→Queirolo took effect)
- **Lecouvreur** — only street-address society/news items
- **Killarney** — no renaming context beyond Feb. 21
- Phrases: **"changing the names of certain streets"** → veto, Oct. 26,
  Dec. 28, + Oakland Tribune May 8/12/15 legal ad — that is Oakland's own
  "Ordinance changing the names of certain streets in the city of Oakland"
  (verified; ruled out). **"an ordinance changing the names"** (Jan.–Jun.) →
  only the Feb. 2 article. **"street names"** swept Feb.–Apr.: all 20 hits
  identified and accounted for (list = the permalinks above).

CDNC's only Los Angeles daily for 1897 is the Herald (the Times, Evening
Express, and Record are not in CDNC for that year).

**Where the full text most likely lives:**

1. **L.A. City Archives** (Erwin Piper Technical Center): the signed ordinance
   (approved Feb. 26, 1897) in the ordinance books, and council minutes for
   Feb. 1–2 and Feb. 23, 1897. The definitive copy.
2. **Los Angeles Times, Feb. 3 and Feb. 24, 1897** (ProQuest via LAPL — not
   CDNC): the Times covered the same sessions and may have printed the list or
   long extracts.
3. **The city's legal-advertising paper.** Publication was charter-required for
   the ordinance to take effect. Suggestive: on Feb. 8, 1897 the council opened
   bids for city advertising — Evening Express 17¢/inch, Record 19¢, Herald
   24¢, Times 35¢ (see the Feb. 9 article). If the contract moved to the
   Express or Record, the legal printing is in a paper CDNC lacks (LAPL/CHS
   microfilm).
4. **The Burks map** (Directory Co., Mar. 1897, 38×[4]5 in., 50¢) and the 1897
   Los Angeles city directory — both encode the complete old→new data.
5. **Bernice Kimball, "Street Names of Los Angeles" (BOE, 1988)** — compiled
   from the city's own change records; LAPL holds it.

## Items — commission report as presented Feb. 1 (Herald Feb. 2, LAH18970202.2.34)

Summary-level, verbatim where quoted:

- ✔ "Pearl street has been changed to Figueroa street, being a continuation of
  that thoroughfare" (applied: Figueroa Street entry)
- ☐ IN COVERAGE (Boylston St): "the Figueroa street in the northwest hills has
  been changed to De La Guerra street" — superseded at the Feb. 23 hearing by
  **Boylston street** (see banked Feb. 24 article); already cited inside the
  Figueroa entry's origin line
- ⏸ Highland Park: thoroughfares "will be designated as 'ways,' commencing at
  J street, which is designated as Way Fifteen, and continuing to the northeast
  city boundary, which is Way Thirty-seven" (Feb. 21 report converts Ways →
  Avenues 36–45 / 50–58)
- ⏸ Pico Heights: "Lettered streets in Pico Heights have been done away with
  … and suitable Spanish names have been provided" (petition of several
  hundred residents)
- ⏸ Vernon district: "The avenues in the recently annexed Vernon district,
  from A to F, have also been done away with and suitable names provided"
- Context: "By the recent annexation some seventy-five duplicates were brought
  into the city"; commission aimed "to perpetuate historic personal names …
  discoverers, founders, missionaries, American pioneers and old settlers";
  Spanish titles chosen to be "appropriate, musical, not too long, and not
  easily mispronounced." A corrected map with changes in red ink accompanied
  the report.

## Items — joint session Feb. 18 (Herald Feb. 19, LAH18970219.2.24)

Actions taken at the session, verbatim extents kept. Where these duplicate the
Feb. 21 final report, the Feb. 21 wording (already banked) governs.

- ✔ "Short street was changed to Fourth street" † (dup of Feb. 21 item, applied)
- ✔ IN COVERAGE (4th St): "Second street east of the river was changed to
  Fourth street" (ex-Mayor W. H. Workman's request — Boyle Heights; the
  original ordinance had made Boyle Heights' Second St "Lugo street," which
  Workman protested)
- ✔ IN COVERAGE (5th St): "Fourth street east of the river was changed to
  Fifth" (same Workman group)
- ⏸ "The name of Mono street, which had been changed to Third, was restored"
- ⏸ "Keno street was changed to Killarney street" [Feb. 21 prints "Kino"]
- ✔ Pearl→Figueroa UPHELD over ex-Mayor J. R. Toberman's protest ("he had the
  street named Pearl when there were not more than half a dozen people living
  on it … over twenty years ago"); committee said the change "had caused more
  favorable comment from citizens at large than any other one act" (color for
  Figueroa/Pearl entry — Toberman as Pearl's namer is a citable claim)
- ⏸ First avenue → [ordinance had: Primero street] → compromise **Arlington
  avenue** ("the protestants asking that they be given an American name")
- ☐ IN COVERAGE (Marion Ave): "The name of Marion avenue was restored from
  Stilson place" — i.e. the Feb. 2 ordinance had renamed Marion Ave →
  "Stilson place"; restored Feb. 18. Citable footnote for the Marion Avenue
  entry: it nearly ceased to exist in 1897.
- ⏸ East side system: Washburn's motion "that streets commencing with Water
  street, the first east of the river, be changed to avenues, and be numbered
  consecutively, commencing with Water street as Avenue Eighteen, and
  continuing to the northern city boundary, the last being Avenue Fifty-eight"
  — carried 4–3 over Lummis, who wanted to start at Avenue One (Nickell: east
  siders "objected to being set off as a little country town"). Origin story
  of the Lincoln Heights/Montecito Heights numbered Avenues.
- ⏸ "Hawkins street be changed to Beatrice avenue … Hawkins street was
  changed, but the committee afterwards changed this to Manitou street"
- ⏸ Pritchard street → "Conservatory drive" proposition FAILED
- ✔ IN COVERAGE (Central Ave): "Wolfskil avenue and Vine street, south of
  First, were changed to Central avenue, and Vine street north of First was
  left." [Feb. 21 report words it "Vine street, from Second to First → Central
  avenue" — consistent; NB Vine also continued north of 1st and kept its name]
- ⏸ "Orange avenue and Dana street were changed to Compton avenue"
- ⏸ "Alcantara to Taylor street"
- ⏸ "Manuella to Pomona street" [= Manuela]
- ⏸ "Eugarde to Ontario street" [OCR; = Ugarte, per Feb. 9 + Feb. 21]
- ⏸ "E=enlon to Dara street" [OCR garble — see conflict note below]
- ⏸ "Chlcote to Webster street" [= Chicote]
- ⏸ "Saragassa to Rossini street" [OCR; = Zaragosa — see conflict note below]
- ✔ IN COVERAGE (Georgia St): Georgia Bell street → Nevada street dispute
  (Major Horace Bell vs. Gen. Forman, at length) referred to the full council;
  **resolved Feb. 23 as "Georgia street"** (LAH18970224.2.15); Bell accepted
  and dropped his court threat (LAH18970227.2.12)

### Dated primary claims inside Forman's Feb. 18 statement (for Georgia St / Delong St)

- ✔ IN COVERAGE (Georgia St): "Nevada" was the tract-map name — Downey Harvey
  tract (recorded Aug. 2, 1886) and Dunnigan tract (recorded Sept. 10, 1886)
  both name the street Nevada. "Nevada street from Rouland street to Eleventh
  street, and from Ninth street to Tenth street (some 3000 feet or more) was
  changed to Georgia Bell street" **May 10, 1889**; "Georgia street from
  Washington street to Rouland street was changed to Georgia Bell street on
  September 11, 18[9?]0" [OCR "is«n"; verify year in council records].
  So the 1897 "compromise" name Georgia was itself one of the street's
  pre-1889 names. Bell's daughter-street claim: named for his wife Georgia
  Bell by an earlier council.
- ✔ IN COVERAGE (Delong St): "the name of De Long street from Virginia … was
  done by City Engineer Dockweiler, and was in honor of the Arctic explorer"
  [George Washington De Long] — corroborated by Dockweiler in person at the
  session. Former name Virginia street was, per Bell, for his daughter
  Virginia. Undated (pre-1897); citable as the Herald Feb. 19, 1897 report.

### OCR conflict flag (carried over from omnibus-1897-renaming.md)

Feb. 19 prints "Escalon → Dara" + "Saragassa [Zaragosa] → Rossini";
Feb. 21 prints "Escalon → Rossini" + "Zaragosa → Dana." One article has the
pairs swapped (both garble Dana/Dara). The Feb. 21 final report is the later
and controlling document, but the signed ordinance text is needed to settle it.
