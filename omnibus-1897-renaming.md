# Omnibus: the 1897 citywide street-renaming

**Document:** Los Angeles Herald, "Talking Retrenchment" (final report of the
special committee on changing street names), Feb. 21, 1897.
**Permalink:** https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=LAH18970221.2.28
**Companion:** Herald, Feb. 24, 1897 (council hearing on this report — Boylston,
Georgia, Court, Miramar, Manitou items): https://cdnc.ucr.edu/?a=d&d=LAH18970224.2.20

## The big picture (context worth citing)

A commission spent six weeks revising street names citywide; its ordinance made
**over 350 changes**, against "barely a dozen protests." Committee's stated
findings: changes were made to remove duplicates or unify one name along a
street's length; over two-thirds of new names English, under one-third Spanish;
resulting city total ≈ 1,250 English vs. 150 Spanish street names (a
quantifiable anglicization moment — potentially map-worthy someday).

**⚠ THE FULL ORDINANCE (350+ changes) IS NOT IN THIS ARTICLE** — this is only
the committee's ~50 amendments to it. Finding the full ordinance text (likely
printed in the Herald as a legal notice, or in Council records, ~Feb.–Mar.
1897) is the highest-value single research target for this project. See
research-leads.md.
